    EZ Runs This should be slow enough that you can chat with a friend or be in tune with the nature that is around you.


    Intervals This is only for experienced runners who have not been battling any injuries. Intervals should help you improve your VO2 max, which will make your body more efficient on race day. You will run the distance prescribed (ie 800m), recover, and start again. It's preferred that your recovery be an EZ jog that gives you just enough time to be ready for the next one. I personally like around 2-2.5 minutes, or around 400m. So, for 800m intervals, you'll run 800m fast, 400m slow, and repeat. Make sure you get at least the day's full mileage. A day that says "8 miles with 8x800m" would require about 2 miles of warmup/cooldown in addition to the 6 miles that 8x800m (with 400m recoveries) would provide.

    You should run fast enough during the interval that the first bit of your recovery distance still seems a bit taxing. This will teach your body to regather strength while still expending some energy and will pay dividends on the final miles of your race. You should, however, keep your pace slow enough that your first, middle, and final intervals are at the same pace. 400m ~= quarter mile 800m ~= half mile 1600m ~= one mile (Note: if you are training for the Marathon and running 800m intervals, see Yasso 800s)


    Hills This is only for experienced runners who have not been battling any injuries. Find an incline that is about a quarter mile long. The group train meets to run the Sharon Wood hill that's closest to the park entrance. Your overall speed will be slower than doing 400m intervals but the effort/stress level should be about at a 400m interval level. Try to leave enough in the tank to run your first, middle, and final hills at the same pace. This workout should feel difficult but not to the point that you would dread doing them again.


    Tempo This is only for experienced runners who have not been battling any injuries. There are a lot of different theories and ideas of what a Tempo run means.
    Overall Time 30 minutes 35 minutes 40 minutes 45 minutes
    EZ warmup time 10 minutes 10 minutes 15 minutes 15 minutes
    Gradually approach Tempo 10 minutes 15 minutes 20 minutes 25 minutes
    Tempo Pace for the prescribed "# at tempo" minutes
    Gradually slow back down to EZ for the remaining minutes


    Race Pace This is only for experienced runners who have not been battling any injuries. Some runs can be augmented with a certain number of miles being at race pace. This is the speed you believe you'll be able to match come race day. If only part of a run is to do be done at race pace, let it be near the end of the run.
